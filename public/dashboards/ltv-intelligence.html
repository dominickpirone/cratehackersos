Whale vs Tire Kicker — the two dossiers

🐋 The Whale (target relentlessly)

$1K+ LTV · 5.7% of buyers · 36% of revenue · stays ~4 years
  • 48 years old, 22 years DJing (median) — the most veteran segment
  • Open Format (72%) wedding/corporate/mobile DJ, 11–50 gigs a year
  • Weekend Warrior or Empire Builder avatar; 51% part-time solo, 25% full-time
  • Already pays for music: multiple record pools — DMS (2×) and Promo Only over-index
  • VirtualDJ over-indexes 2× (23.6% vs ~12%); Serato flat across tiers
  • Found via Facebook ad (50% of stated sources) or YouTube video — not IG/TikTok
  • Skipped the trial: only 19% entered on $2.50 trial; bought full price or lifetime day one
  • 27% attach add-ons (VIP, Rekord Cloud); 10% own Banger Button; Hacker Hotel buyers cluster here
  • Goal language: multi-op, hiring, $100K, weddings & corporate — business builder
  • 93% United States · NPS +58

👻 The Tire Kicker / Ghost (filter out)

<$50 LTV · 44% of buyers · ~3% of revenue · gone in month one
  • ~40 years old, ≤10 years DJing; more beginners & "just starting out" (24%)
  • Club/bar/bedroom identity rises as LTV falls (club: 15% of ghosts vs 5% of whales)
  • Single-genre: Hip-Hop (21%) and EDM over-index; Open Format drops to 49%
  • No record pool (21% of ghosts) — doesn't yet pay for music, won't pay for tools
  • Rekordbox over-indexes 2.6× among ghosts (16.7% vs 6.4% whale) — club-software signal
  • Entered on the $2.50 trial (58–63%) — the trial funnel is the kicker magnet
  • Ghosts: 56% refunded; NPS +22; average 0.1 rebills — they never activated
  • Goal language: festivals, EDC, Ibiza, "get famous" — the stage dream, not the business
  • International over-indexes (UK/CA/DE ~2× whale rate) — FX price pain from the survey
  • Avatar overlap: Open-Format Dreamer + parts of Joy Protector

Targeting Playbook — point the money at whales

Seed & audience moves

Meta LAL seedStop seeding lookalikes from ALL buyers. Seed from the 2,104-whale list (value-based custom audience with LTV column) — Meta optimizes toward 48-year-old US wedding/corporate pros instead of trial-hunters.
Value optimizationSwitch key campaigns from purchase-count to VALUE optimization; upload LTV as conversion value so the algorithm learns the whale shape.
ExclusionsExclude the 5,577 ghost/refunder emails from all prospecting AND retargeting. They are pure CAC burn with a 56% refund rate.
Geo & ageUS-first budgets; 35–60 age band for whale campaigns. International: keep, but expect ~½ LTV or localize pricing.

Offer architecture (the trial is a kicker magnet)

The finding63% of kickers and 58% of ghosts entered on the $2.50 trial vs only 19% of whales. Whales self-select by paying full price or lifetime on day one.
The moveShow annual/lifetime FIRST to whale-profile traffic (FB, 35+, wedding/corporate creative). Reserve the trial for cold TikTok/IG traffic where it belongs — or gate it behind a quiz that routes business-builders to the real offer.
Attach engine27% of whales buy add-ons. Post-purchase flow: VIP add-ons + Banger Button + Hacker Hotel to every new full-price buyer within 30 days.

Creative selection = buyer selection

Whale magnetsWedding/corporate scenes, 45-year-old on camera, "multi-op", "your second DJ is only as good as your crates", must-plays/do-not-play liturgy, time-back-to-family, business math ($ per gig).
Kicker magnets (use only for cheap trial CAC)Festival/EDC imagery, "get famous", club-dark aesthetics, EDM drops, hype editing. These pull 40-year-old dreamers who refund.
Qualifying copyAdd friction that filters: "For working DJs with 10+ years of chaos on their drives." Whales have 22 years of tenure — say the number and kickers scroll past.
Survey-takers skew valuable (matched mean LTV $295 vs $227 population): the people who answer your surveys are disproportionately your best customers — keep surveying, and treat survey completion itself as an LTV signal.

🚨 LTV compression — the headline finding: a 2022 buyer was worth $206 at 12 months; a 2025 buyer is worth $106 — down 49%. Month-1 value fell from $85 to $46. New-buyer volume is up 48% YoY but each buyer is worth half as much. This is the arithmetic signature of the trial-funnel mix shift (see LTV Intelligence tab: trials = 63% of kickers). Volume is masking value decay — the single most important conversation for CMO + CFO.

Three recoverable leaks, sized

Dunning68,912 failed payment events all-time; only 21% recover within 30 days. June hit a record 2,006 events. Industry-standard dunning (pre-expiry card updater, retry ladder, SMS) recovers 40–60%. Every extra 10 pts ≈ $4–6K/mo at current MRR. (Note: events include retries — dollar sizing needs invoice-level dedupe, specced in handoff.)
Cancel saves15,703 cancellation requests all-time, 658 reverted = 4.2% save rate. Price point 38 ($15/mo save offer) exists but is barely converting. A real save flow (pause option, downgrade, seasonal hold — remember "Monthly pricing when DJing events is seasonal" from the survey) at a 15–20% save rate ≈ $15–25K/yr MRR preserved per point.
Refunds$18K in June (9.0% of revenue), and annual plans refund at 27% — the highest of any entry class. Hypothesis from survey objections: the "trap page" annual upsell buys refunds. Test consent-clear annual checkout vs forced-annual flow.
📋 The one-slide summary for the exec meeting: Revenue is fine ($200K June, +36% YoY). The machine underneath is not: MRR flat 30 months, cohort LTV down 49% in 3 years, trial entries produce 0.3% whales, and three leaks (dunning 21% recovery, 4.2% save rate, 27% annual refunds) are worth more than the next creative test. Priorities: (1) entry-offer mix shift, (2) dunning + save flow, (3) whale-seeded acquisition from the LTV tab.
