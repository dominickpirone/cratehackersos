Intro offer → full price, by offer type (matured cohorts) · blended 44%

Kartra flags any discounted first charge as a "trial" — nobody's on a free trial; everyone pays ($1–$15 intro). This measures who survives to the first full-price rebill. The 56% who don't = the 35-day cancel cliff. Note: the $2.50 July 4th sale (price point 8) isn't in this metric yet — add pp8 to the trial definition at the next refresh.

The leaky-bucket problem: June added $31.2K new MRR but lost $29.1K to churn — net just +$2.1K. That's ~$80K/mo of ads to run in place on MRR. The highest-leverage fix isn't more ads — it's lifting the 44% intro-offer→full-price rate and cutting ~17% monthly revenue churn (a chunk is recoverable failed payments via dunning/card-updater).

What's working

  • June beat plan on the CMO sheet — $207.5K gross / $190K net (top-line), above the $200K target even with a lighter final week.
  • Past $2.09M ARR. MRR $174.2K, up ~$20K since mid-May — the milestone hit well ahead of the Q3 plan.
  • Banger Button nearly doubled — 788 → 1,490 active in six weeks; BB MRR $12.5K. A real second product line (but June contribution was negative — fix the unit economics as it scales).
  • LEVEL11 at 49 subs / ~$4.1K MRR at $99/mo. High-ARPU tier working; first rebills mid-July.
  • Day-1 economics stayed ~breakeven (Net CAC +$1.64) even as spend scaled — customers largely pay for their own acquisition.

What to watch

  • Real profit went slightly negative in June (−$7.1K QuickBooks) after May's 3% margin. Growth is deliberately funded from margin — make that a conscious monthly decision, not drift.
  • The leaky bucket: +$31.2K new MRR vs −$29.1K churned. GRR 83.1% and the 44% intro-offer→full-price rate are the two highest-leverage numbers in the company.
  • Cash buffer is thin — $23.3K operating cash vs $76.7K card balances. June's settling deposits (~$25K) help in early July.
  • Processing fees ~$9K/mo and rising (blended 4.33%). Steering volume to Stripe or negotiating rates at ~$1M/yr volume is real money.
  • ~90% paid-ads dependency — the single biggest structural risk; diversification is the strategic priority.
