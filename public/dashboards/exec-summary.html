Recommendation: the cohort number ($105) is what your customers actually paid in their first 12 months. The formula ($138) is what your current ARPU + churn would predict. The gap is because formula uses today's higher-paying ARPU pool, while cohort includes the ~43% of signups that cancel inside 35 days. Pick cohort for board-grade conservatism; pick formula for unit-economics modeling. Either way, the old $136 figure is still defensible.

LTV refresh — what changed

  • Cohort 12-month LTV (CH+BB blended): $105. Based on 5,775 customers whose first sub started May 2024–May 2025. This is the realized first-12-month revenue per customer, averaged across all signups (not just retainers).
  • Formula 12-month LTV: $138. Driven by current ARPU $22.78 and blended monthly churn ~13.6%. Essentially flat vs your published $136.
  • Why the cohort number is lower: ~43% of new CH subs cancel within 35 days. The cohort method captures these zero/low-LTV customers; the formula method back-tests against today's higher-paying active base.
  • Average CH subscriber lifetime: ~5 months for those who have churned (median just 2 months — heavily right-skewed); ~7.5 months implied from current 13.3% monthly churn. Currently active CH subs have averaged 24+ months of tenure.

What to watch

  • Margins compressed to 3% in May. Record $157.9K revenue produced only $4.8K net income — Facebook alone was $50.8K and total expenses hit $150.9K. June MTD is similar ($88K rev / $1.1K net). Growth is being funded by reinvestment; the call to make consciously is how much profit to trade for scale.
  • Processing fees ~$7K/month and rising. Blended 4.34% (PayPal 4.86% vs Stripe 4.20%). $35K YTD. Steering volume toward Stripe, or negotiating rates at this scale, is a real lever.
  • Failed payments climbing with volume. $20.9K / 449 txns in the last week (up 15% WoW). Confirm the dunning/recovery rate is keeping pace.
  • Blended CAC jumped to $52.66 in the Jun 8–14 week (from ~$35), pulling LTV:CAC down to 3.21x. Ad spend scaled to $25.4K that week — still Day-1 profitable, but the efficiency ratio is worth watching if spend keeps outrunning new-sub revenue.
